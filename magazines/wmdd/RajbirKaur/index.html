TAJ MAHAL

The Taj Mahal is a tomb built in the 17th century by the Mughal king Shah Jahan in memory of his wife, Mumtaz Mahal. Eventually, Shah Jahan was entombed in the Taj Mahal with his wife. The building is in the city of Agra, Uttar Pradesh.The tomb is the center piece of a 17-hectare, which includes a mosque and a guest house, and is set in formal gardens bounded on three sides by a strong wall.

MISTY FOG

Mont Avic is a mountain in the Graian Alps, in the Aosta Valley

, north-western Italy. It has an elevation of 3,006 m. It is included in the Gran Paradiso massif. The peak is located on the boundary between the common part of Champdepraz and Chambave, and gives its name to a regional natural park called Mont Avic Natural Park Parco Naturale del Mont Avic (It) / Parc Naturel du Mont-Avic.

CHINATOWN

The first area in London known as Chinatown was located in the Limehouse area

of the East End of London. At the start of the 20th century, the Chinese population of London was concentrated in that area, setting up businesses which catered to the Chinese sailors who frequented in Docklands.

TRAVEL
Camden Town, London
Camden Town often shortened to Camden, ambiguously also used for the much larger London Borough of Camden of which it is the central neighborhood. It is an inner city district of northwest London. It is one of the 35 major centers identified in the London Plan.
Big Ben, England
Big Ben is the nickname for the Great Bell of the clock at the north end of the Palace of Westminster in London. The tower is officially known as Elizabeth Tower, renamed to celebrate the Diamond Jubilee of Elizabeth II in 2012. Previously it was known simply as the Clock Tower.
Mont Blanc, Italy
Mont Blanc or Monte Bianco both meaning White Mountain, is the highest mountain in the Alps and the highest in Europe after the Caucasus peaks. It rises 4,808.73m above sea level and is ranked 11th in the world in topographic prominence. The mountain lies in a range called the Graian Alps
Piazza del Duomo ,Italy
Piazza del Duomo is the main piazza of Italy. It is named after the Milan Cathedral. The piazza marks the center of the city in a geographic sense and because of its importance from an artistic, social point of view. A major twist in the piazza's evolution came about after the Second Italian Independence war.
